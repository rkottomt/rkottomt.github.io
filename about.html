About

I'm Rohit, an Electrical and Computer Engineering student at Carnegie Mellon University (Dean's List, Class of 2029). I grew up at the #1 STEM high school in the country and arrived at CMU with a deep interest in the hardware-software boundary.

My work spans optics hardware, machine learning, and systems engineering. I've done research on beehive acoustic anomaly detection with an IIT ECE professor, built ML classification models at Pfizer, and helped launch the first high school chapter of Hack4Impact. This summer I'm joining Nokia Bell Labs as an Optics Hardware Intern, contributing to a prototype development project with Google.

Outside of engineering I think a lot about energy systems and environmental research — the intersection of physical infrastructure and climate. I'm also drawn to low-latency systems, quantitative finance, and anything that requires reasoning carefully under uncertainty. I'm open to roles in optics, hardware, and quant-adjacent engineering. If you're building something at the edge of the physical and computational, reach out.
